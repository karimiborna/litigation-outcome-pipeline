Other: The Court DISMISSES WITHOUT PREJUDICE this action on procedural grounds. This Court is not passing judgement on the merits of the case. By way of analysis, the Court notes the following:

1.) Petitioner's action is for breach of contract, but no contract was filed with the Petition.
2.) The court is persuaded by Respondent that the claims brought in this action are part of a larger business dispute between the parties. The parties agree that there are two other actions pending between them - one in Alameda County and one in San Mateo County. From what the parties describe, the San Mateo matter is an unlimited action where Petitioner has sued Respondent for breach of the contract involving the sale of a business that gave rise to the specific project that is the subject of this Small Claims action. Responded has countersued Petitioner for, among other things, fraudulent inducement.

--- PAGE BREAK ---

Both parties are represented by counsel in the San Mateo matter. Accordingly, this Court finds that to prevent inconsistent verdicts, convenience of the parties, procedural fairness, and judicial economy, the present dispute should be addressed in the larger San Mateo action.
