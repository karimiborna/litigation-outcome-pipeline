Minh Luong v. City and County of San Francisco

CSM-25-870033

This matter was heard Thursday, October 2, 2025 at 3:30 pm in Department 506 of the above-entitled court. Plaintiff Minh Luong appeared in person and testified under oath. Defendant City and County of San Francisco appeared through its representative, Ashley Carnegie. Both sides presented documents for the court's review.

On the motion of Plaintiff, and for good cause, the court set aside its previous order dismissing the matter without prejudice. The court heard evidence and argument from both parties with respect to the timeliness of the claim underlying the case and with respect to the merits.

As to the timeliness issue, the court finds that Plaintiff filed within the time period provided for in Government Code § 945.6. In the alternative, the court finds that there is a factual basis on which to conclude that applicable time limits are subject to equitable tolling.

On the merits, a careful review of the record reveals that Plaintiff has established by a preponderance of the evidence that Defendant is responsible for the loss of Plaintiff's property valued in excess of the jurisdictional limit in Small Claims Court during the execution of an unscheduled and unannounced sweep of the encampment at which Plaintiff was living.
