MOTION TO VACATE JUDGMENT

--- PAGE BREAK ---

I. Facts

The address at which plaintiff claims service was made, 857 Columbus Avenue, San Francisco, California 94133, is the commercial address of defendant's business "Church of the Air-Cooled," an Porsche repair business. The hours of the business are 11:00 a.m. - 7:00 p.m. However following the birth of their daughter, Milda Jansone, in November 2024, defendant's work schedule changed to accommodate his duties as a father. On a typical Wednesday, defendant would remain at home at 15 San Antonio Place, San Francisco, California, with his daughter and his son, Otto, until his wife, Magda Zala Jansone, returned home from work in the afternoon to take over the care of the infant daughter. On the day of the alleged service of the notice of the small claims hearing, July 30, 2025, this arrangement was followed by defendant and his family. At the time of the supposed service on July 30, 2025, 10:22 a.m., defendant was not in the business at 857 Columbus Avenue. At that time on that date, defendant was still at home in the company of his son Otto Jansons, and the young infant daughter. So defendant was never served with any papers at that time and date. Needless to say, defendant missed the hearing on plaintiffs small claim, and plaintiff was awarded a judgment in defendant's absence.

II. Law

Under both Code of Civil Procedure §§116.730 and 116.740, a defendant who failed to appear at the hearing may move to vacate the judgment.

III. Argument

Here defendant was never served in accordance with Code of Civil Procedure §116.740. According to the Declarations of defendant Jansons, Otto Jansons, and Magda Zala Jansone, defendant Christopher Jansons was not in his business at 857 Columbus Avenue, San Francisco, California at 10:22 a.m. on July 30, 2025.

Defendant points out that plaintiff's August 12, 2025 proof of service of plaintiff's Claim and Order for Hearing lacks any description of the person served. This deficit makes impossible the independent verification that service was made by the process server.

2.

--- PAGE BREAK ---

IV. Conclusion

In this case, defendant was never served with the plaintiff's claim and order to go to hearing. For this reason, defendant's motion should be granted.
