Gaurav Ahlawat v. Essex Property Trust, Inc.

CSM-25-870311

Background

This matter was heard Friday, October 3, 2025 at 3:30 pm in Department 506 of the above-entitled court. Plaintiff Gaurav Ahlawat appeared and testified on his own behalf. Defendant Essex Property Trust (“Essex”) appeared through its representative, Marrisa Hernandez. Both parties introduced documents into the record for the court’s review.

Plaintiff’s claim for $7,000.00 is based on allegations that Essex, and/or its management company, MB360, has breached its obligations with regard to his tenancy at its property located at 1200 4th Street, San Francisco, CA 94158 (“the Property”) thereby breaching the implied warranty of habitability. Plaintiff is a tenant of Essex pursuant to a written Lease Agreement (“Lease”) between the parties. At material times, Plaintiff has occupied Unit 609 on the sixth floor of the Property.

Also before the court is another claim by Mr. Ahlawat and the claims of several other sixth floor tenants:

Cabrera v. Essex Case No. CSM 25-870310 (claim = $10,596.10)
Ahlawat v. Essex Case No. CSM 25-870312 (claim = $9,000.00)1
Galan v. Essex Case No. CSM 25-870314 (claim = $12,500.00)
Otanes v. Essex Case No. CSM 25-870315 (claim = $12,500.00)
Lee v. Essex Case No. CSM 25-870317 (claim = $12,500.00)

Plaintiff’s Allegations

In this case Plaintiff Ahlawat alleges that Essex breached the warranty of habitability by:
(1) failing to timely remediate “severe mechanical noise and vibrations in >50% of the apartment, (2) loss of habitability and (3) management took more than 6 weeks to address the noise issue during which I couldn’t use most of the apartment.”

Mr. Ahlawat testified that severe noise and vibration from building equipment situated on the roof above his apartment unit substantially interfered with his quiet enjoyment of the property. On or about May 7, 2025 he submitted a service request seeking repair of the machinery in order to eliminate the intrusive noise. Over the course of the next month, efforts

--- PAGE BREAK ---

were made to discover the source of the noise and vibration. On more than one occasion, management asserted that the noise was within normal limits. Finally, on or about June 12, 2025 the HVAC system in question was replaced entirely and the problem was resolved.

Defendant’s Response

Ms. Hernandez testified to the efforts engaged in by management to alleviate the situation and produced maintenance logs in support of her assertion that defendant took reasonable steps to take care of Mr. Ahlawat’s concerns as quickly as possible. Among other things, she noted that the tenant had been offered a transfer to another unit or the option to move out, terminating the lease without penalty.

Conclusion

Plaintiff has met its burden to show that the noise and vibrations he experienced during the period of time in question rendered the apartment in which he lived partially, although not entirely, uninhabitable. At the same time, Defendant has demonstrated what appeared to be sincere and good faith efforts to remedy Plaintiff’s problem. Based on this record, the court finds that Plaintiff is entitled to damages as set forth.
