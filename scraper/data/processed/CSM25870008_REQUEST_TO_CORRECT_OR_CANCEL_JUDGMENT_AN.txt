Attachment to SC-108 - Section 3(b)

Case No.: CSM-25-870008
Plaintiff: Marvin Matamoros

I attended the small claims hearing in this matter, but I respectfully request that the judgment entered on November 3, 2025 be vacated for the following reasons:

1. Material facts and evidence were not fully considered.
At the hearing, the court did not have before it the full set of documentary and photographic evidence demonstrating long-standing habitability and safety issues at 310 Otsego Ave. The record therefore does not reflect the extent or seriousness of the substandard conditions that gave rise to my claim.

2. New or previously unavailable evidence exists.
I possess photographs, short video clips, maintenance requests and responses, text and email communications with the defendants, and contemporaneous notes documenting repeated failures to address hazardous conditions. These items were not presented or were not available for the court's review at the original hearing. I am prepared to submit these materials for the court's consideration.

3. Prejudice and impact on health and safety.
The unaddressed conditions materially affected the habitability of the unit and had a demonstrable impact on my health and safety. Because the court did not consider the full evidentiary record, the resulting judgment is materially unfair and does not reflect the merits of the claim when all relevant facts are considered.

4. Request for relief and fairness.
Vacating the judgment is necessary to correct the incomplete record and to afford both the court and the parties a fair opportunity to resolve the disputed facts. I request that the court vacate the judgment, reopen the case, and schedule a new hearing at which I may present the full evidence described above and any witnesses necessary to support my claims.

5. List of proposed exhibits.
If the court grants this motion I will offer the following exhibits at the new hearing:
- Photographs showing unsafe and substandard conditions (dated).
- Short video recordings documenting hazards and repairs (dated).
- Copies of written maintenance requests and defendants' responses.
- Relevant text and email communications.
