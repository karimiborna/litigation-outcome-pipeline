I. BACKGROUND

On November 4, 2025, Appellant City and County of San Francisco filed an appeal of the
Small Claims judgment entered in Respondent's favor. Notice of the appeal trial was
received approximately five weeks before the scheduled December 11, 2025 trial date,
spanning the Thanksgiving and December holiday periods.

Having prevailed in both Small Claims proceedings, Respondent is committed to
presenting this matter thoroughly in Superior Court and requires adequate time to prepare
properly.

II. GOOD CAUSE EXISTS FOR CONTINUANCE

A. Learning Superior Court Procedures

--- PAGE BREAK ---

This is Respondent's first experience with Superior Court civil proceedings. Respondent is still learning these procedures and requires adequate time to properly utilize them.

Respondent is also researching the potential applicability of 42 U.S.C. § 1983 civil rights claims and wants to respect the Court's time and procedural expectations. If proceeding pro se, Respondent will use the additional time to ensure efficiency and proper presentation.

B. Witness Coordination and Subpoena Preparation

Respondent intends to subpoena and call as witnesses:
- The HSOC sweep supervisor whose conduct is central to both incidents alleged in this case
- HOT team workers who initiated placement in the PSH program and made clear assurances about the safety of Respondent's property
- Department of Homelessness and Supportive Housing personnel who coordinated with sweep teams during the January 18, 2024 incident, including those who made phone calls to sweep supervisors while Respondent was signing his housing lease

Respondent needs adequate time to learn how to properly prepare and serve subpoenas, coordinate witness availability, and prepare for witness examination.

C. Document Discovery and Evidence Preparation

Respondent intends to request and review:
- Healthy Streets Operations Center and Department of Public Works records regarding the December 21, 2023 and January 18, 2024 operations
- Communications between City departments, particularly between HSOC, DPW, and housing services regarding the January 18, 2024 incident
- Sweep scheduling and authorization documentation
- Department protocols for encampment operations
- The full text of the federal court injunction in Coalition on Homelessness v. City and County of San Francisco to understand how it pertains to the allegations and identify potential violations during the events in question

I. Judicial Efficiency and Finality

Adequate preparation time serves judicial efficiency. Under California Code of Civil Procedure §116.780, the Superior Court's judgment after trial de novo is final and not appealable by either party. Given this finality, both parties should have adequate opportunity to present their strongest cases on the merits.

A continuance allows Respondent to:

- Obtain Small Claims case records
