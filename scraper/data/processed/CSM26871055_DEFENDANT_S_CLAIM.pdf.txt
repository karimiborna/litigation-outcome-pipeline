DECLARATION
(This form must be attached to another form or court paper before it can be filed in court.)

Plaintiff and Defendant have been co-master tenants on the master lease for 2957 Washington Street, San Francisco, California, since January 2024. On December 27, 2023, Defendant paid the landlord the full master lease security deposit of $6,995 on behalf of both parties. On December 31, 2023, Plaintiff paid Defendant $3,947.50, representing Plaintiff's share of the master lease security deposit. In January 2024, Plaintiff and Defendant jointly entered into sublease agreements with three subtenants, who collectively paid $3,850 in security deposits. These funds constituted subtenant security deposit funds held in trust, not personal funds of either master tenant.

On January 22, 2024, Defendant transferred $1,800 to Plaintiff, at Plaintiff's request, as part of subtenancy deposit balancing. These funds originated from subtenant security deposits and were not reimbursement of Plaintiff's master lease deposit interest. There was no agreement that subtenant security deposits would offset or satisfy Plaintiff's interest in the master lease security deposit.

Plaintiff vacated the unit on January 7, 2026, while the master lease remained in full effect. The landlord confirmed in writing that the lease remained in full force and effect, would not be revised during an active tenancy, that all master tenants remain liable until lease termination, that the master lease security deposit remains held by the landlord until final vacancy, and that the landlord does not hold or administer subtenant security deposits.

At the time Plaintiff vacated, Defendant did not agree to buy out Plaintiff's lease or security-deposit interest using Defendant's personal funds, as the master lease remained in full force and effect and Defendant continued to bear ongoing legal and financial risk and potential liability under the lease, including unresolved lease-related issues arising from Plaintiff's actions prior to vacating the unit, for which Defendant remained jointly responsible.

Despite multiple written requests, Plaintiff failed to acknowledge or transfer the subtenant security deposit funds she retained. As a result, Defendant was required to refund one subtenant's security deposit using Defendant's personal funds, while Plaintiff continued to retain $1,800 in subtenant security deposit funds.

Defendant seeks a determination that the master lease security deposit is held by the landlord and will be refunded to Plaintiff and Defendant upon lease termination, subject to lawful, itemized deductions. Defendant further seeks judgment against Plaintiff for $1,800 for wrongful retention of subtenant security deposit funds, plus allowable court costs, prejudgment interest from January 7, 2026, and any other relief the Court deems just.
