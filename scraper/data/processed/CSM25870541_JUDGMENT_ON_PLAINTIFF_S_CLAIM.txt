**THE CLAIM**

This matter, filed on September 29, 2025 and amended on November 4, 2025, was heard on November 25, 2025 in Department 506 of the above-entitled court. The Plaintiff, Eda Karagoz, asks for damages in the amount of $12,500.00 based on allegations of negligence in connection with her tenancy at 3201 Octavia Street, Apt. 102 in San Francisco.

All parties appeared in person. Defendants SF Multifamily JV II Property Owner, LLC and SF Multifamily Pools 2-3 B appeared through their respective representatives. The Court heard the sworn testimony of the parties and reviewed the documents submitted by each party.

By agreement among the parties, Defendant SF Multifamily JV II Property Owner, LLC, predecessor in interest to SF Multifamily Pools 2-3 B, is dismissed from the action.

**FACTS**

Plaintiff's claim for damages is based on two events: (1) leaking water and steam from a defective radiator in her room on or about November 20, 2024, and (2) the theft of her automobile about a week later, on or about November 28, 2024.

Plaintiff occupied a residential unit at 3201 Octavia Street in San Francisco pursuant to a sublease, executed on or about July 28, 2024, which binds her to the terms of the Residential Tenancy Agreement ("Lease") executed by co-occupants and master tenants on or before July 9, 2024. The Lease contains language requiring all tenants to carry renter's insurance (¶ 46) and to hold the Owner harmless for any personal property loss (¶ 47).

Radiator

The evidence reflects that on November 20, 2024, the gasket connecting the steam radiator in Plaintiff's room to the building's plumbing failed at a time when Plaintiff was not home. The gasket failure caused a steam and water leak that damaged Plaintiff's personal property.

The apartment's other residents (the master tenants) attempted to notify the Owner's property manager of the leak by texting and calling his work cell phone shortly after they noticed the leak at or around 7 PM. They did not shut off the valve themselves, nor did they utilize the after-hours emergency contact number provided by the Owner.

Plaintiff testified that she came home approximately three hours later to find her room flooded and full of hot steam. She then used the emergency contact information to notify the building manager, Matteo Ortiz Fischer, who arrived at the apartment shortly thereafter to stop the leak by shutting off the radiator's valve.

The following day, November 21, 2024, the radiator was repaired and the property inspected. Humidifiers were installed for a period of four days to dry out the room. Plaintiff was given a rent credit the amount of $213.11 as well as a hotel refund of $311.81 for the night of the incident.

Plaintiff made a claim with Lemonade, her insurance carrier, for her loss. The record reflects that the carrier paid a total of $5,469.69, apparently disallowing part of her claimed damages. Plaintiff testified that she pursued the matter with Lemonade to no avail.

Car Theft

Plaintiff's automobile was stolen on November 28, 2024 by an unidentified individual who apparently gained access to the building through the front gate and, having done so, was able to enter Plaintiff's apartment and take her keys which were apparently hanging on a hook somewhere close to the apartment's front door. Neither the locksmith hired by the Owner to change the locks nor the San Francisco police officer who was dispatched to the scene on the 28th noticed any sign of forced entry to the apartment door, leaving open the possibility that it had not been locked.

Shortly before the theft, on September 30, 2024, Plaintiff had notified the Owner that the front gate was not closing properly. Mr. Fischer testified that the gate was repaired on October 1, 2024.

Plaintiff's car was not recovered until approximately two weeks later, at which time it was towed to the San Francisco Municipal Transit Authority lot and impounded. **DISCUSSION**

Having carefully considered the evidence, the court finds that Ms. Karagoz has failed to meet her burden of proof. First, the terms of the Agreement, including Paragraph 46 (renters insurance) and 47 (hold harmless) protect the Owner from liability for Plaintiff's loss except under circumstances, not found here, in which a landlord is grossly negligent or violates an applicable statute or regulation. Second, Plaintiff has not shown that the Owner's responses to either of the two incidents at issue in this case were anything other than reasonably prompt, good-faith efforts to remedy unfortunate but unforeseeable harm. That being the case, the Owner has no duty to pay damages to Plaintiff, who has recovered on insurance claims with respect to both events.

Plaintiff is not owed any money by Defendant.
