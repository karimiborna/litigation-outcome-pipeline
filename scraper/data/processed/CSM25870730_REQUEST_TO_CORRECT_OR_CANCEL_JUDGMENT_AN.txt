**SC-108 Request to Correct or Cancel Judgment and Answer (Small Claims)**

**Request**

This form is used to ask the court to correct or cancel a small claims judgment. You must file this form no later than 30 days after the clerk mailed Form SC-130, Notice of Entry of Judgment. Filing this form does not extend the deadline to file an appeal.

If you are the person asking the court to correct or cancel a judgment, fill out (1)-(5) on this page, then file it at the clerk's office. The clerk will mail a copy of the form to all other plaintiffs and defendants in your case. The court will give the other plaintiffs and defendants at least 10 days to answer this Request. The court will mail its decision to you or tell you to go to a court hearing. If you did not go to the trial, you must use Form SC-135, Notice of Motion to Vacate Judgment and Declaration.

If you receive this form, read below, then fill out (6) through (9) on page 2. The court will mail its decision to you or tell you to go to a court hearing.

**DECLARATION**

(This form must be attached to another form or court paper before it can be filed in court.)

At my small claims hearing which took place on January 20th, 2026, I presented my case, outlining a pattern of neglect and inaction by Bridge Property Management which left me no choice but to move out. During my case, I -attempted- to play 8 very short (<1 min. ea.) recordings of my neighbors abused and screaming dog (a sound that I was subjected to for 20+ hours each week, over months). These recordings, when accompanied by their dates and times, were meant to prove a pattern of harm done to me over a very long period of time. Harm that could have been avoided if Bridge simply moved me to another unit. After hearing ten seconds of the first recording, the Judge declined to listen to my remaining recordings, or allow me the opportunity to properly build my proof (I must insist that you review the courtroom recording of my hearing in order to verify this fact). Yesterday I received an Entry of Judgment (SC-130) that states that I did not meet the "legal burden to show that the conditions of the property operated by the defendants made that property uninhabitable or that the defendants failed to respond reasonably to [my] complaints". I am filing this form now because: if I did not meet the legal burden of proof, it is because the Judge denied me the opportunity to do so. In the interest of justice, I believe this judgment must be cancelled so that I can argue my case in front of a judge who will let me present my proof -in full- before deciding whether or not I meet the burden of proof.

California Civil Code Section 1927 grants tenants the right to "quiet enjoyment" - a right which I -attempted to- prove that I was denied, but which the Judge declined to hear my arguments on.

California Civil Code Section 1941.1 requires landlords to maintain a trash-free building, a responsibility which I proved Bridge was neglectful, and which the Judge overlooked.

Furthermore, on January 3rd, 2026 I received, in the mail, a form SC-112A which has a signed declaration from Devin Fleming stating that he is -not- a party to this case. I was very rupised to see him standing alongside Carrie Jeffords and representing Bridge Property Management Company on January 20th, and when I conveyed this surprise to the Judge, she dismissed that as well.

Whether or not this judgment is cancelled, I remain homeless, so I will continue to pursue every avenue of justice that I can, because Bridge Property Management's refusal to reasonably respond and resolve basic habitability issues (with very basic solutions, I must note) has cost me my life.
