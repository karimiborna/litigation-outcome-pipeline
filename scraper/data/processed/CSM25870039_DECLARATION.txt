SC-100, ITEM 3 - EXPLANATION OF AMENDED CLAIM

Plaintiff: Camillia Saouaf

Defendant: Li "Cathy" Wen Bu

Note: This is an amended complaint that increases the amount of the original. No other changes have been made to the original filing.

I rented the apartment at 731 Chenery Street, San Francisco, from April 8, 2024, to April 30, 2025. At the end of my tenancy, the defendant withheld $3,152.97 from my $3,972.47 refundable deposit.

These deductions were improper under California Civil Code Section 1950.5.

The following deductions were made:

- $1,852.97 for utilities, which were never invoiced monthly during the tenancy as required by the lease agreement. Retroactive billing at move-out violates Civil Code Section 1950.5(b).

- $1,000 for painting, allegedly done by an individual with no valid license match on the California State License Board database. Painting due to normal wear and tear (e.g., nail holes or minor scuffs) is not deductible under Section 1950.5(e).

- $300 for cleaning, attributed to a business that cannot be verified. The unit was left in broom-clean condition, and the cleaning quote I received from a cleaner, recommended by the landlord, ranged from $150 to $180.

Legal violations and lack of documentation:

- I was never given photo evidence of any damage before or after the alleged work was completed.

- No receipts or valid invoices were provided for the painting or cleaning work.

- These failures violate Civil Code Section 1950.5(g), which requires landlords to provide itemized statements and documentation, including photographs and licensed receipts when available.

Additional Issue:

- The defendant claimed in her final statement that a refund check for $819.50 was mailed on May 21, 2025. As of the date of this filing, I have not received any refund. (6/24/25)

I Am Seeking:

- Return of the full refundable deposit: $3,972.47
- Plus 2x statutory damages under Civil Code §1950.5(l): $7,944.94
- Total claim amount: $11,917.41
