MC-025

SHORT TITLE: Bierbrodt v Greystar California, Inc. CASE NUMBER: CSM-25-870165

ATTACHMENT (Number): A

(This Attachment may be used with any Judicial Council form.)

Plaintiff suffered a serious raw sewage event in his apartment that lasted five days. He sued Defendant Greystar California, the admitted management company fiduciary to the property owner. Although Defendant resolved the issue fairly quickly, Plaintiff had to vacate his unit for five days until the problem was abated. He also missed two days of work dealing with the problem. The Court finds that Plaintiff suffered compensable damages and makes the following award:

1. Loss of Use of Unit: Plaintiff pays $2,122 in rent a month, which equates to $70.73 a day. Plaintiff was damaged in the amount of $353.65 ($70.73 x 5) for five days of lost use. (Plaintiff stayed with friends and did not incur any additional rental cost for the nights the unit was uninhabitable.)

2. Lost Wages: Plaintiff lost two days of work at $439 per day, for a total of $878.

3. Meal Expenses: Plaintiff claims $500 in meals for the five days but provided no receipts. The Court finds that a meal expense of $75 per day is more reasonable and awards $375 in meal expense for the five days.

The above three categories of damages total $1,606.65. The Court also awards $65 in court costs.

Judgment for the Plaintiff in the amount of $1,671.65
