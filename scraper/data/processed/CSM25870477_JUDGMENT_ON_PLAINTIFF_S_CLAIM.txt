**ORDER AFTER HEARING**

This matter, filed on September 12, 2025, was heard on December 8, 2025 in Department 506 of the above-entitled court. The Plaintiff, Lindsey Johnson, asks for damages in the amount of $12,500.00 for, among other things, "wrongful termination and unpaid wages."

Ms. Johnson appeared in person and testified under oath. Defendant The Red Jack Saloon appeared through its co-owner, Laurie Fusia, who also testified under oath. The Court received and has reviewed documents from each party.

**FACTS**

The Plaintiff worked as a bartender at Defendant's establishment for a total of 22 shifts between June 18 and September 30, 2023. She testified that she was regularly required to work well past the bar's closing time and that she was not paid for doing so. She complained about this and other matters to the owner, Ms. Fusia, in an email dated October 1, 2023. She was fired a few days later on October 4, 2023.

In early November 2023, after her termination, she emailed the Bay Area Enforcement Office of the California Alcoholic Beverage Control Department to complain about being "forced" to sell alcoholic beverages after hours. The California ABC closed its file in the matter with no action. Ms. Johnson did not file a complaint with any government agency about her wage claim or her termination, nor did she apply for unemployment benefits. She testified that she got another job immediately.

**DISCUSSION**

Plaintiff has shown that on five occasions, August 27, September 10, 17 and 27 and October 1, 2023 she worked 2 to 3 hours past the end of her shift without compensation. The record also clearly reflects that she was terminated because she complained to her employer about her working conditions.
