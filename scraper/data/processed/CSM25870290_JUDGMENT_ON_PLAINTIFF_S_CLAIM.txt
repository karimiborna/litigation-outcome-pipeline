Speed v. Essex Management Corporation

CSM-25-870078

This matter was heard on Monday, September 29, 2025 at 8:30 am in Department 506 of the above-entitled court. Plaintiff A. Jonathan Speed appeared via Zoom along with his spouse and co-tenant, Silvia Meredo. Defendant Essex Management was represented by its Community Relations Manager Brehyonnia Reeves. Plaintiff and Ms. Reeves each testified under oath and presented documents for the court's review.

Plaintiff and his spouse were tenants at Defendant's Bryant Street rental property in San Francisco which they occupied pursuant to a written lease first entered into in September 2003. The lease was terminated on an unspecified date in 2025. Plaintiff seeks damages in the amount of $12,500.00 arising from Defendant's alleged failure to timely remediate infestation of the rental unit by cockroaches between approximately June 18, 2024 and August 31, 2024.

Another dispute between these parties concerning mold remediation in the same unit was resolved at a Small Claims Court hearing held in this Department on July 10, 2025, with a stipulated payment by Defendant to Plaintiffs. (Speed, et al. v. Essex Management, CSM-25-869898.)

Claimant indicated at the hearing in this matter that he intends to present yet further claims in this court based on three other disputes with Essex concerning the same rental unit (failure to timely return a security deposit, damages arising from alleged theft and damages arising from an alleged incident of arson).

Summarizing the evidence in this matter, on or before June 18, 2024, Plaintiff reported an infestation of cockroaches in the apartment, asserting that the unit was rendered unsafe as a result. Defendant's response was logged into the company's maintenance log on June 24, six days later. The following day, June 25, the apartment was treated by Defendant's pest control vendor. That same day, Plaintiff notified Essex of "roaches in the dishwasher." On July 1, Defendant responded with an offer to perform a "full fog treatment," on or about July 8. The vendor seems to have elected for various reasons to re-spray instead of performing the fog treatment. On July 9, the vendor reported having done so, and that "I did not see any live or dead activity in the unit."

The parties continued to disagree about the efficacy of Defendant's efforts at remediation. Plaintiff demanded replacement of one or more kitchen appliances. He also filed a report with the City and County of San Francisco. The matter escalated throughout July and August 2024, with Plaintiff retaining counsel who presented a demand letter to Essex on this and other issues. At one point, Defendant offered a rent

--- PAGE BREAK ---

credit which was declined by Plaintiff. Finally, on or about August 31, 2024, the cockroach issue was resolved to the satisfaction of Plaintiff.

None of the basic facts with regard to the infestation and its remediation were contested in this matter. Plaintiff agrees that the issue was finally fixed, but argues that delays in eradicating the roaches rendered the unit uninhabitable for a material period of time. Defendant asserts that its efforts were reasonable under the circumstances, ultimately successful and, in any event, compliant with its obligation under the lease and applicable law. Essex also relies on language in the lease by which Plaintiff purportedly "releases and waives any right to sue Landlord for claims resulting in any physical injury, illness or economic loss which Resident may suffer as a result of or incidental to pests or insects at or near the Property." (Lease at Section 14(e).)

By its delay, Defendant breached its duty to provide a habitable dwelling to Plaintiff and his spouse for several weeks during the months of July and August 2024. The court finds that Plaintiff has not waived his claim by operation of the quoted provision of Section 14(e) of the lease. (See, Cal. Civil Code Section 1942.1.) In awarding damages, the court takes into account Defendant's efforts to remediate, the delays and repeat treatments inherent in the pest control process and Defendant's good faith offer to waive rent.

The court also finds that damages awarded to Plaintiff for this period of time would not be duplicative of those awarded in Docket No. CSM-25-869898, as that award was based on a stipulation between the parties concerning allegations covering a time period prior to that in this case. No opinion is expressed as to the preclusive effect of the judgment in this matter on other claims Plaintiff apparently intends to pursue.
