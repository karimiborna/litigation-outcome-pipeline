**ORDER AFTER HEARING**

**THE CLAIM**

This matter, filed on September 23, 2025, was heard on November 25, 2025 in Department 506 of the above-entitled court. The Plaintiff, Chai Tha Yeng Yang, asks for damages in the amount of $12,500.00 as a partial refund of payment made to Defendant Shahin Javaheri, M.D. in connection with plastic surgery performed at his Oakland, California facility on or about November 11, 2022. Ms. Yang alleges that the negligently performed procedure failed to resolve "concerns I raised about the "prominent misalignment of the columella [the external end of her septum] to the philtrum [the vertical groove between the central base of her nose to her upper lip]."

Both parties appeared in person. The Court heard the sworn testimony of the parties and reviewed the documents submitted by each party.

**FACTS**

On October 26, 2022, Ms. Yang consulted with Dr. Javaheri about the possibility of cosmetic surgery on her nose. She alleges that her "primary goals were to achieve facial symmetry, especially in the columella and nose base, and to improve nasal height." She testified that her nostrils are somewhat asymmetrical due to a congenital condition.

At the consultation, Dr. Javaheri took photographs of her face and nose and used 3D imaging to show her the possible outcome of the surgery. He testified that he showed her the photographs and the 3D imaging and "explained to her that the right nasal Ala [nostril] was unlikely to change but may be improved. He explained that "this was likely a congenital abnormality."

Ms. Yang agreed to engage Dr. Javaheri to perform "Rhinoplasty w/ ear cartilage graft" and "mid face implant" for a total payment of $19,475.00. The written quote to which she agreed stated that the procedure would be performed on November 11, 2022. The procedure was performed on that date.

Ms. Yang testified that she was not satisfied with the results of the procedure and that she experienced pain and discomfort after the procedure. She also testified that she had to return to Dr. Javaheri's office several times for follow-up appointments and that she was charged for each of these appointments.

Dr. Javaheri testified that he performed the procedure in accordance with the standard of care and that he did not deviate from the standard of care in any way. He also testified that he informed Ms. Yang of the risks and benefits of the procedure and that she signed an informed consent form before the procedure was performed.

**RULING**

Based on the evidence presented, the Court finds in favor of the Plaintiff, Chai Tha Yeng Yang, and against the Defendant, Shahin Javaheri, M.D. The Court awards the Plaintiff damages in the amount of $12,500.00, which represents a partial refund of the payment made to the Defendant for the procedure.

The Court also orders the Defendant to pay the Plaintiff's costs and fees associated with this action.

This order is final and binding on all parties.

Ms. Yang's complaint, although framed as a claim for a refund on a contract, appears to rest on the premise that Dr. Javaheri's treatment of her fell below the standard of care in his profession. In that regard, Dr. Javaheri has provided the November 5, 2025 declaration of Dr. Rex E. Moulton Barrett stating, among other things:

Dr. Javaheri "provided non-negligent care before during and after surgery and without harm to the patient. Ms. Yang's now and new/post-surgical claim that she did not achieve greater symmetry should not be considered a breach is [sic] the standard of care."

DISCUSSION

Statute of Limitations

Dr. Javaheri asserts that Ms. Yang's claim is barred by the applicable statute of limitations for professional malpractice. Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 340.5. Ms. Yang, however, has neither pled nor proven malpractice. She sues for what she refers to as a "refund" for services performed unsatisfactorily. As such, the limitations period for breach of a written obligation (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 337) would appear to apply here, rather than that for professional negligence.

Breach of Contract

Construing the Ms. Yang's complaint as a claim for breach of contract, and after careful consideration of the evidence, the court finds that Ms. Yang has failed to meet her burden of proof. Note is taken of the provision in Dr. Javaheri's quote with respect to "Revision Policy."

Plaintiff is not owed any money by Defendant.
