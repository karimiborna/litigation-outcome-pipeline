Brian Headen v. Pricilla Williams

CSM-25-870361

Appearances

This matter was heard Tuesday, October 7, 2025 at 3:30 pm in Department 506 of the above-entitled court. Plaintiff Brian Headen appeared via Zoom. Defendant Pricilla Williams appeared in person. Each party testified under oath. Defendant Williams filed a written response to Plaintiff's claim, seeking its dismissal on various grounds.

Plaintiff's Claim

Plaintiff is currently incarcerated at the California State Prison - Corcoran, an institution within the California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation (CDCR) in Corcoran, Kings County, California. Plaintiff is not a San Francisco County resident, nor does the record show that he ever has been. None of the acts of which he complains took place in this county. Ms. Williams, the sole named Defendant, is a Correctional Counselor II employed in Kings County by the CDCR. Among her other duties, she is responsible for facilitating the appearance via Zoom of incarcerated individuals at court hearings in legal proceedings in which they may be involved.

In his claim filed on August 18, 2025, Mr. Headen sues Ms. Williams as an individual. He specifies that he is not "suing a public entity." (Response to SC-100, Question 8.) He alleges that Defendant lives or does business in San Francisco County (Question 5), although he states that her address is "4001 King Ave, Corcoran, CA 93212" (Question 2). In response to Question 9 on the claim form, Plaintiff states that he has "not filed more than 12 other small claims in California within the last 12 months." His claim for $12,500.00 in damages is based on the following allegations:

Defendant did knowingly, willfully and maliciously violate the plaintiff's First Amendment rights by denying him [all] access, materials, and assistance to/from law library, disregarding her (administrative regulations) policy & requirements, pursuant to federal law; then did retaliate for the plaintiff telling her he was going to sue her but she filed false report, causing plaintiff loss of liberty, property (rights) and privileges, violating 42 USC section 1983 and the Tom Bane Civil Rights Act.

At the hearing, Plaintiff provided no evidence with respect to his access to the law library or lack thereof. Rather, he testified that Defendant "maliciously" retaliated against him, allegedly because of his prior threat to sue her, by interfering with his ability to appear by Zoom in a

--- PAGE BREAK ---

significant number of Small Claims Court cases he has recently filed. The record does not reflect whether any of these cases were filed in Kings County; Plaintiff testified that he has filed claims in other “non-prejudiced” counties, including San Francisco, San Mateo, Sacramento, and Los Angeles, where he contends the courts have more “experience” with litigation by incarcerated individuals. (San Francisco Superior Court records reveal two other filings by Mr. Headen in addition to this case: Headen, et al. v Dan, et al., Case No, CSM-25-870324, and Headen v. Torres, et al., Case No. CSM-25-870460.)

Mr Headen does not claim that his access to Zoom has been denied in this case or in either of his other San Francisco Small Claims Court matters, but he did testify that he has been denied his right to appear via Zoom on at least 14 occasions since December 23, 2024 in other counties. He asserts that Ms. Williams is responsible for some or all of these cases being dismissed on account of his failure to appear at scheduled hearings.

Plaintiff’s testimony identified no conduct by Defendant outside the scope of her official duties as a Correctional Counselor II. Nor did he provide evidence as to how Defendant’s alleged wrongdoing resulted in damages of $12,500.00 or any other amount.

**Defendant’s Response**

Ms. Williams testified that she does not reside in this county, nor did she take any action of any kind in San Francisco involving the Claimant. She stated that as part of her official duties she has been involved in attempting to facilitate what she describes as an “onslaught” of recent out-of-county small claims filings by Plaintiff. Ms. Williams claims that in each such instance she or others responsible have made reasonable efforts to make Plaintiff available for Zoom hearings and that neither she nor anyone else has intentionally interfered with his access thereto. She also stated that Plaintiff has not been denied access to the prison law library.

Ms. Williams asks the court to dismiss plaintiff’s claim on several grounds: (1) improper venue, (2) governmental immunity, (3) failure to exhaust administrative remedies, (4) Plaintiff’s federal 42 U.S.C., Section 1983 claim is filed in the wrong court, (5) lack of merit and (6) no proof of damages.

**Conclusion**

1. Plaintiff’s claim is subject to dismissal because venue is not proper in San Francisco County. The facts giving rise to his claim occurred in Kings County. Plaintiff is housed in a correctional facility in that county. Defendant and all others with firsthand knowledge of the facts comprising the claim are located in Kings County. Plaintiff’s disingenuous allegation that Defendant “lives or does

--- PAGE BREAK ---

business" in San Francisco County" is belied by the statements in his own claim and is, in any event, not supported by any evidence. His contention that venue is proper pursuant to Cal. Civ. Code § 52.2, allowing actions under the Bane Act to be brought in "any court of competent jurisdiction," is misplaced; the statute addresses small claims court jurisdiction, not venue. Plaintiff's forum shopping in this county (and in the other counties in which he has apparently filed) is unauthorized by law.

2. Plaintiff has made no showing that Ms. Williams acted outside the scope of her official duties as an employee of the CDCR, and Plaintiff has neither alleged nor proven facts on which Ms. Williams can be sued personally. She is accordingly immune from suit. Cal. Gov't. Code §§ 815, 815.2. Plaintiff's contention that he may sue Ms. Williams personally under Section 52.1(n) of the Bane Act (Cal. Civ. Code § 52.1(n)), providing exceptions to governmental immunity for certain claims, is without merit. None of the exceptions applies here. See, Cal. Gov't. Code §§ 821.6 (injury caused by prosecution of administrative action against prisoner), 844.6 (injury to prisoner) and 845.6 (injury due to failure to furnish medical care). The claim is subject to dismissal on this ground as well.

3. Plaintiff's complaint is also subject to dismissal because he has neither alleged nor proven that he has exhausted, or has been excused from exhausting, the administrative remedy provided by the Department of General Services Government Claims Program (CGP). See, discussion above and Cal. Gov't. Code §§ 945; 945.4, 945.6.

4. Although Plaintiff cites to 42 U.S.C. § 1982 in his complaint, he also appears to rely on the Bane Act, a California statute as a basis for relief. The court has jurisdiction of the matter on that basis.

5. On the merits of the claim, Plaintiff made no attempt to demonstrate that he was denied access to the law library as he alleged in his complaint. His testimony with respect to access to Zoom court hearings - credibly controverted by Defendant - wholly fails to establish that Defendant purposefully (or in any way) interfered with his rights.

6. Finally, Plaintiff has made no showing that any of the conduct of which he complains led to any monetary loss to him.

Having carefully considered the evidence and arguments submitted by the parties, the court orders that the complaint herein be, and the same hereby is, DENIED in its entirety.
