**ORDER AFTER HEARING**

This matter, filed on October 14, 2025, was heard on December 3, 2025 in Department 506 of the above-entitled court. The Plaintiff, Carlos Morales, asks for damages in the amount of $2,000.00 in earned but unpaid wages.

Mr. Morales appeared in person and testified under oath with the assistance of a Spanish language interpreter. Defendant Ace Roofing SF, Inc. appeared through its owner, Young C. Chon, who also testified under oath. The Court received and has reviewed documents from each party.

The Plaintiff testified that he had worked for Defendant as a roofer at various times over the course of the last two years, that he was terminated from this employment in or around September 2025 and that he was given a final paycheck which he was unable to negotiate because it was improperly made out. He also stated that he had performed work for approximately four days in September for which no payment has been tendered. Defendant admitted that the check in question was not negotiable, but asserted that Mr. Morales could have contacted him to cure the defect. Defendant also testified that Plaintiff had been paid all wages owed and that Mr. Morales had been terminated for cause.

The Court finds that Defendant failed to comply with applicable provisions of California wage law at the time of Mr. Morales' termination. Plaintiff is awarded the damages he seeks.

**IT IS SO ORDERED.**
