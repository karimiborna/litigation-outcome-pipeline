ORDER AFTER HEARING

**THE CLAIM**

This matter, filed on September 30, 2025, was heard on December 1, 2025 in Department 506 of the above-entitled court. The Plaintiff, Robert Stewart, asks for damages in the amount of $12,500.00 arising from the allegedly unconcented to and unlawful recording of all or portions of Mr. Stewart's medical appointments at a Kaiser Permanente ("Kaiser") facility in the City of San Francisco on December 24, 2024, January 30, 2025 and February 25, 2025.

Mr. Stewart appeared in person. The Defendant, Dr. Erica Kurien, failed to appear. Kaiser representative Reginald Thompson sought to testify and/or argue on behalf of Dr. Kurien. Permission to do so was denied. Mr. Stewart was sworn and testified in his own behalf.

The court takes note of its own records in Case No. CSM-25-870166, Stewart v. Kurien, filed on July 11, 2025. In that case, Stewart made identical claims against Dr. Kurien concerning appointments he had with her at Kaiser on March 23 and April 8, 2025. That case was heard on September 16, 2025. Both parties appeared and testified under oath on the merits of Plaintiff's claims. Judgment was entered in favor of Defendant and against Plaintiff the same day.

Plaintiff's Request to Correct or Cancel the Court's Judgment was denied on October 9, 2025.

**CHALLENGE**

Plaintiff initially indicated that he declined to consent to a hearing before a "visiting judge." Although the Court permits parties to refuse to accept pro tem judges (lawyers who are designated, trained and sworn to act in place of regularly sitting judges), no such right to decline to consent applies to visiting judges. The Court so advised Plaintiff.

Mr. Stewart then sought to mount an oral challenge for cause under Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 170.1. Plaintiff's statements in support of his challenge were primarily directed at the judicial officer who heard his prior case (and against Kaiser and its representative). No facts remotely suggested that the judicial officer who heard this case was biased or prejudiced against Plaintiff or in favor of Defendant. The Court denied Plaintiff's challenge for cause.

**JUDGMENT**

For the reasons stated above, IT IS HEREBY ORDERED, ADJUDGED AND DECREED that judgment be entered in favor of Defendant and against Plaintiff. The Clerk of the Court is directed to enter judgment accordingly.
