The Judge did not allow the plaintiff to address it whatsoever. The defendant's own testimony provided clear evidence of a violation of these laws, which was not properly considered by the court.

Statement of Facts and Applicable Law

1. Violation of the California Invasion of Privacy Act

During the hearing, the defendant, a doctor at Kaiser Permanente, admitted to recording two confidential patient-doctor conversations with the plaintiff using an AI annotation tool named Abridge. When questioned by the court, the defendant explicitly stated that she "did not recall" asking for consent to record the conversations or to use the AI tool. This statement is, in effect, an admission of guilt.

This action is a direct violation of the California Invasion of Privacy Act, under California Penal Code § 632. This law makes it a crime to intentionally record a "confidential communication" without the consent of all parties. A patient-doctor conversation, by its very nature, is a confidential communication.

Furthermore, the defendant's use of Abridge also violates California's AB 3030. This law specifically requires health providers to ask permission for any kind of recording, especially AI-generated communications.
